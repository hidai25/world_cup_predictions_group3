Overview

Billions around the globe watch the FIFA world cup. Given the popularity of each of the games played during the tournament, people have attempted to predict outcomes of this world popular sport. The FIFA world cup is played every four (4) years and three (3) years leading up to it, all the teams play qualifying matches that allows them to participate in the final round except the hosting nation which is automatically qualified. After this initial qualification phase, 32 teams including the host nation are qualified and play for the title over a period of approximately a month. Using machine-learning techniques, the goal is to predict the outcome of all 64 games (including the playoff games) of the FIFA world cup 2018.

The Stages of an Asset Bubble

  1. Stealth phase: In this phase, a small community of hobbyists know about the asset and its potential. They are those who understand the emerging opportunity and act accordingly with big purchases at a low price of the asset. Generally, they do so very quietly. These are habitually called “smart money investors”.
  2. Awareness Phase: The momentum is being noticed and many investors join the purchasing spree.This phase can be accompanied by sudden price drops. At this time, “smart money investors” strengthen their positions.
  3. Mania Phase: The general public notices the price increase following large media coverage and they start buying in large quantities. This, in turn, causes very fast price increase in a short period of time. At the end of this phase, “smart money investors” start to liquidate their positions at a large profit .
  4. Blow-Off phase: Generally there is some trigger moment that causes the general public to think that something has changed. The decline in price start off very slowly. As the sell-off speeds up, investors panic, and prices reverse course and descend much faster than they increased.

Causes

  • Low interest rates are commonly a very strong contributing factor to an an asset bubble. Investors can borrow cheaply and they don't receive a good return on their fixed income type investment assets. Therefore, investors look for yield and thus invest in higher risk higher return ass. In the long term It makes prices in different asset reach abnormal leveles.
  • Sometimes an asset class become very popular among the investor community. Prices of this asset class soar and create a “herd” behavior amongst investors and the public. Nevertheless, economics indicators don’t always reflect this isolated behavior which make policy-makers overlook it.
  • The classical economic law of supply and demand apply to situation where the supply of a certain asset/commodity is shrinked suddenly. This make investors buy that asset following a belief that supply will shrink even more in the future. It makes the price of this asset skyrocket.
